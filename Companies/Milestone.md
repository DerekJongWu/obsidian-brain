## Business Description

- **Problem:** Cyber advisory is dominated by Big Four + Accenture, who staff junior analysts at $20/hr and bill them at $400–500/hr. Customers universally dissatisfied — slow, low-quality, expensive — but lack alternatives.
- **Product:** AI-first consultancy that replaces the human-heavy model for pure advisory work (reports, posture assessments, compliance audits — NOT implementation). Handles:
  - **Objective data:** system logs, policies, configurations.
  - **Subjective data:** meeting transcripts, organizational dynamics, board / management / system inconsistencies.
- **Wedge:** AI-generated posture / compliance / audit reports for CISOs and IT audit departments at 5,000+ employee regulated companies — pay-per-outcome first, expand to license / continuous assessment.

## Market & Competition

- **Market size:** $28–40B global cyber advisory market. Deloitte alone reported ~$15B in cyber consulting revenue in 2024.
- **Incumbents:** Big Four (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) and Accenture — dominant share, low NPS.
- **Differentiation:** AI-native delivery of advisory deliverables — same output (or better) at fraction of the labor cost. Can ingest both objective system data and subjective organizational/transcript data to surface inconsistencies between board presentations vs management vs actual systems — a capability traditional consultancies cannot scale.

## GTM & Business Model

- **Buyer persona:** CISOs and IT audit / internal audit departments at 5,000+ employee companies in regulated industries (financial services, healthcare, pharma). Sweet spot: companies under the Fortune 500 with multiple annual advisory engagements.
- **Two service models:**
  1. **Pay-per-outcome projects** — posture assessments, compliance audits (one-off engagements).
  2. **License model** — continuous assessment / always-on tool for internal audit departments.
- **Pricing:** Not disclosed in this meeting beyond model framing.
