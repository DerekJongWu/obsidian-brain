## Business Description

- **Problem:** CFO-office workflows (AP, bill pay, expense management, ledger reconciliation) are manual and run on pre-LLM stacks bolted with AI.
- **Product:** Mili.ai — AI super agent automating bill pay, expense management, ledger reconciliation, corporate cards, ACH/wire/stablecoin payments via chat/voice prompting.
- **Wedge:** Accounts payable / Mili.ai. Future agents planned for AR, bookings, procurement.

## Market & Competition

- Incumbents (Ramp, Brex, Amex) built pre-LLM and bolting AI on; Level is AI-native from day one.
- Ramp has ~2% market share; market not winner-take-all.
- Brex recently sold to Capital One.

## GTM & Business Model

- Three revenue streams:
  1. Platform/subscription fee ($500–$20K/month depending on size), includes a token allotment
  2. Token consumption (usage-based, above the base allotment)
  3. Payments interchange and transaction fees
- Blended take rate modeled at 2–3% of TPV; higher margins on tasks using fine-tuned open-source models (Mistral etc.)
- Base fee provides SaaS floor with NRR upside from heavy users (addresses LP preference for subscription stability)
- Bank partnerships in progress for card issuing, bill pay, and stablecoin; avoiding exclusivity.
