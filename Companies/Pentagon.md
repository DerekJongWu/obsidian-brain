## Business Description

- **Problem:** Multi-agent and human-agent collaboration today is bolted onto chat tools (Slack, Teams) that weren't designed for persistent, event-triggered agents or shared company-wide knowledge. Slack requires its enterprise plan just to expose the API needed to integrate a company brain.
- **Product:** Multiplayer desktop app — shared workspace for human employees and AI agents.
  - Agents are persistent, event-triggered; they can DM each other and humans, and self-organize into group chats.
  - Agents have roles, tools, and handoff capability (to other agents or humans).
  - Every user builds a "company brain" (knowledge repo) passively in the background.
- **Wedge / entry point:** Two arms:
  - **Self-serve Pentagon Studio** — public launch 2.5 weeks ago (as of 2026-06-09).
  - **Forward-deployed engineering (FDE)** — ~2 weeks old (as of 2026-06-09). Beachhead = data enrichment agents, embedded custom with larger customers.

## Market & Competition

- **Slack comparison:** Pentagon is designed from the ground up to integrate company brains; Slack requires its enterprise plan just to expose its own API for this.
- **Customer selection criteria (as of 2026-06-09):** Tangible outcomes achievable within 3 months, AI-progressive teams, clear use case.

## GTM & Business Model

- **Two-arm motion (as of 2026-06-09):** self-serve (Pentagon Studio) + forward-deployed engineering (FDE).
- **FDE pricing (as of 2026-06-09):** Started at $5K/mo, moved to $10K/mo. Target = "1-1" (1-year, $1M contract) with larger enterprises.
- **FDE 3-month implementation arc:**

  - Month 2: implementation
  - Month 3: outcomes measurement and iteration
