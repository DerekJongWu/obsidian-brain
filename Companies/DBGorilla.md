> [!summary] One-Liner

## Business Description

- **Problem:** Managing and optimizing database performance requires scarce, expensive DBA expertise. Legacy DBaaS providers are reactive and lock customers into specific infrastructure.
- **Product:** AI agents that autonomously monitor, diagnose, root-cause-analyze, test solutions on clones, and implement fixes for database performance issues. Learns system-specific patterns (e.g., "Wednesdays are slow days").
- **Wedge / entry point:** PostgreSQL performance optimization, expanding across database types.

---

## Market & Competition

- **Market size:** DBMS market $170B today, growing to $330B by 2031 (15% CAGR). DBaaS growing 19% CAGR. Structured data growing 50% through 2028.
- **Key competitors:** Legacy DBaaS providers
- **Differentiation:** Proactive vs reactive monitoring; infrastructure agnostic vs vendor lock-in; replaces DBA expertise rather than requiring it.
