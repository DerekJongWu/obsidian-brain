> Spectrum Security is an Israeli/US cybersecurity startup building a detection intelligence platform that automates the security detection-engineering workflow, sitting on top of existing SIEMs to compress the average 121-day threat-to-deployment cycle.

## Thesis Fit

- Detection engineering is a high-value, under-automated layer of the SOC stack
- Non-rip-and-replace approach plays well with regulated/financial customers tied to incumbent SIEMs
- Founder pedigree (ex-Opus Security CEO, ex-Chronicle founding team) aligns with Fin's bias toward repeat security operators
- Fits Fin's security thesis around agentic security and SOC automation

## Business Description

- **Problem:** Average 121-day cycle from threat discovery to deployed detection; SOC detection engineering is manual, fragmented, and stuck across multiple SIEMs and data lakes
- **Product:** Detection intelligence platform that automates security detection engineering — sits between security infrastructure and SOC teams
- **Wedge:** Works on top of existing SIEM infrastructure (no rip-and-replace), allowing customers to gradually disintermediate expensive SIEM costs while controlling detection across multiple data lakes

## Market & Competition

- Distinct from alert triage / SOAR / response — focused on the detection-engineering layer
- Aims to control detection across multiple SIEMs and data lakes, enabling gradual SIEM disintermediation
- ~7 employees (Harmonic, as of 2026-04-28); founder plans to scale to 18–20 next quarter
- Engineering HQ in San Francisco; go-to-market presence in New York

## GTM & Business Model

- Target customers: Organizations with mature SOC teams and detection-engineering needs
- ICP: Large banks, Fortune 20/50 enterprises
- Buyer persona: Detection engineering directors (large orgs) or SOC ops directors (smaller orgs)
- GTM thesis: Best targets are customers that already understand the problem and have a mature SOC
