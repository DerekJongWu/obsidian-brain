## Business Description

- **Problem:** Legacy ITSM tools (ServiceNow, Freshservice, JSM) require extensive customization, lock teams into ticket workflows, and force users out of the tools they actually live in (Slack/Teams). Existing AI add-ons are bolt-ons that don't change the underlying workflow.
- **Product:** AI-native enterprise service management platform that consolidates 2–4 tools and targets 60–70% no-touch resolution. Single point of entry via Slack/Teams for all IT requests. Three-tier agent architecture:
  1. **Service agents** — password resets, app access; dozens available out-of-box
  2. **Autonomous agents** — proactive device monitoring, license management
  3. **Custom agents** — for migrated workflows
- **Wedge / entry point:** Mid-market full-replacement during ITSM renewal cycles; enterprise initial coexistence with ServiceNow with gradual displacement.
- **Functions served:** IT, HR, Legal, Finance — anywhere internal service requests flow.

## Market & Competition

- **Market size:** ServiceNow at $13B revenue; legacy ITSM market is multi-tens-of-billions globally. AI-native competitors (Serval, Console) at <$10M combined ARR (as of 2026-05-18) — wide-open replacement window.
- **Key competitors:** ServiceNow, Freshservice, Atlassian JSM (legacy); Serval, Console (AI-native challengers).
- **Differentiation:** Opinionated product approach — delivers value out-of-box vs requiring extensive customization. Targets broader mid-market vs Serval/Console focus on Bay Area startups.
