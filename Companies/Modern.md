## Business Description

- **Problem:** Traditional ITSM (ServiceNow ~40% share) is painful to use; modern AI-ITSM entrants (Serv, Console) still require 3–6 months of forward-deployed-engineer setup and rely on workflow / playbook authoring. Tickets stall because automation is brittle and pre-coded.
- **Product:** AI-native ITSM platform that spawns a **long-lived agent per ticket**. Agents proactively reach out to team members for missing information, build knowledge graphs from conversations (rather than from pre-authored workflows), and continuously improve. Multi-channel surface: Slack, Teams, WhatsApp, email, voice. Bidirectional sync with ServiceNow, Jira, and other incumbents.
- **Wedge:** Replacing / wrapping ServiceNow in the policy-driven workflow layer (access requests, onboarding, offboarding) with ambient agents that learn organically — removing the need for ticketing UI as the primary user surface.
- **Application monitoring:** Dynatrace integration for server alerts (early integration cited).

## Market & Competition

- Target market: $100–200B IT services
- ServiceNow: ~40% ITSM share, the platform Modern wraps and eventually displaces
- Workflow-based AI ITSM competitors named: **Serv**, **Console** (both require 3–6 month forward-deployed-engineer rollouts)
- Adjacent / prior Fin diligence: [[Atomic Works]], Serv, Console, and currently [[Echelon AI]] (ServiceNow co-pilot)
- Differentiation: long-lived agents vs workflow agents; knowledge graph emerges from conversation, not pre-coded; multi-channel (Slack/Teams/WhatsApp/email/voice) ambient surface vs ticket UI
