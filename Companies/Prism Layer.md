## Business Description

- **Problem:** Enterprise risk assessments in regulated industries take ~8–12 months and ~$2M, and AI-generated risk outputs have been board non-starters because their decision quality lives in a black box (no provenance/audit trail).
- **Product:** AI-native, agentic ERM platform. Sub-agents encode a chief risk officer's methodology plus ~20 years of judgment to reason over inherent risk, controls, residual risk, and risk appetite — surfacing assumptions and trade-offs transparently. Produces decision-grade analysis and a regulator-grade audit binder. Runs over a customer's existing GRC tools and data lakes (AuditBoard, Google Drive) rather than ripping and replacing them.
- **Wedge:** ERM is the entry point; the deeper differentiator is transparent, verifiable reasoning/decisioning that a non-expert (and a board) can audit.

## Market & Competition

- Buyers: Chief Risk Officers, CCOs, CFOs, CLOs; risk function sits under either legal or finance
- Differentiation: only platform doing transparent risk *reasoning* (vs. storage/automation)
- Competitors / adjacent:
  - **GRC tools** (e.g., AuditBoard) — store information; increasingly add automation but don't produce assessments
  - **AI compliance tools** (Vanta, [[Hummingbird]]) — more automation, looking for acquisition targets; Prism Layer in early talks to be their risk-assessment engine (Fin is a Hummingbird investor)
  - **Abacus** (internal wiki / knowledge base with audit trail) — adjacent, not ERM
- Moat thesis: risk experts (not AI engineers) building this; fast-follower risk = another seasoned ERM team copying them
