> [!info] One-Liner
> Chain Reaction is a semiconductor company building chips for fully homomorphic encryption (FHE) — the "holy grail of data privacy" — enabling processing of encrypted data without decryption, positioning as the "Nvidia for the privacy space" with its 3PU (Privacy Processing Unit).

## Thesis Fit

- Addresses massive enterprise pain point: financial services, healthcare, and government organizations cannot move sensitive data to the cloud or use LLMs due to privacy requirements
- Founded by Mellanox co-founder with deep semiconductor expertise and TSMC relationship

## Business Description

- **Problem:** Fully homomorphic encryption has been the "holy grail" of data privacy for decades but was never commercially viable due to computational overhead
- **Product:** Privacy Preserving Processing Unit (3PU™) — custom silicon that enables real-time FHE operations on encrypted data
- **Wedge:** Partnership with TSMC for 3-nanometer chip manufacturing; CRO ran 15% of TSMC revenue for 21 years with office next to TSMC facility
- **Technical approach:** Hardware-focused (chips/cards/systems) vs. software platform; data lakehouse architecture for blockchain and privacy workloads

## Market & Competition

- Primary verticals: financial services, healthcare, government — largest users of sensitive data requiring privacy
- Financial services pain points:
  - Keeping data on-premise due to cloud security concerns
  - Cannot use LLMs due to privacy requirements
  - Morgan Stanley spends $8B annually on data center infrastructure
- Quantum computing threat: bad actors collecting data now for future decryption — Chain Reaction provides immediate privacy solution
- ~105 employees (Harmonic, as of 2026-04-15)

## GTM & Business Model

- **Go-to-market strategy (3 phases):**
  1. Neo-cloud giants (90-day deployment vs. 5-year hyperscaler cycles)
  2. Direct enterprise sales to large financial institutions
  3. Future hyperscaler partnerships (5+ year timeline)
- Hardware sales model: chips, cards, and systems
