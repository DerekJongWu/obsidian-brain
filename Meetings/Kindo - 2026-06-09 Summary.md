## Deloitte's Kindo Adoption

- Evaluated agentic security platforms in late 2024, selected Kindo in **Feb 2025** after testing at RSA.
  - Needed a single platform spanning all **10 cyber domains** (SecOps, identity, AppSec, ERP security).
  - Rejected **Torq** (SecOps only), **Twine** (basic agent builder, too much manual input), and generic platforms.
  - Kindo won on breadth, built-in guardrails, and responsiveness to Deloitte's feature requests.
- Signed a **3-year license agreement in Feb 2026**:
  - **$5M/year** in Kindo licensing, covering ~**100 client deployments**.
  - Base: 2 use cases per client included; 3rd use case triggers consumption-based fees (~10% of revenue).
- **250+ professionals trained** via train-the-trainer rollout (since early April).
  - Not opening platform to all 9,000 staff; practitioners build and deploy, clients don't get direct access.
- Internal "certified to sell" process is the main bottleneck: **6-month cycle, started Feb 2026**.
  - Pipeline includes 40 existing ops clients, several insurance and healthcare accounts.
  - **SOC analyst agent deployment to all threat detection clients expected in ~2 months.**
  - Target: **60 minimum, 100 aspirational deployments by Q1 2027**.

## Alliance Partnership & Revenue Projections

- **Current status:** marketplace partner (co-sell, no co-branding); working toward formal Alliance.
  - Alliance tier allows co-branding, co-marketing, co-developed solutions, and dedicated sales execs with Kindo revenue goals.
  - Deloitte has 3 priority alliances (Palo Alto, SailPoint, Ping — each >$100M) and ~9–10 tier-two alliances.
  - **Kindo expected to enter as an emerging / tier-two alliance; agreement expected in ~2 months.**
  - Historical threshold to sign: $5M run rate; Deloitte confident Kindo will reach it.
- **Deloitte's AI-led transformation practice (US commercial) targets $260M over 3 years:**
  - Year 1 (2026): ~$40M
  - Year 2: ~$110M cumulative
  - Government / GPS practice to be added in year 2, accelerating growth.
  - **Kindo-attributable revenue estimated at ~10% of Deloitte billings on the platform.**
- **Client pricing:** base platform fee (~$250K regardless of module count) plus per-module fees.
  - Example: threat detection module at $250K; identity module up to $1M depending on use cases.
  - Clients see one Deloitte subscription fee; Kindo consumption costs passed through on the back end (same model as CrowdStrike).
