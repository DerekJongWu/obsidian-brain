## Product & Technical Approach

- Identity verification platform using a mobile app for just-in-time biometric verification
  - Combines biometric, document, and geolocation verification
  - Uses cryptographic certificates with chain of trust ("SSL for humans")
  - Novel tech: PG Presence (closes second-screen gap), 3D smartphone data matched with 2D camera data
- Core thesis: Detection will keep falling behind deepfakes, so friction-based solutions are needed
  - Organizations will accept higher friction when fraud-fighting becomes more important than avoiding friction
  - Similar to MFA adoption trajectory
- Current identity stack referenced: Switched from [[Nametag]] to Jumio for North American ID
  - Gap in international founder verification (Pakistan example)
  - Can't distinguish nation-state actors with American mules vs legitimate international founders

## Market Traction & Use Cases

- **Healthcare:** showing strongest adoption
  - Telemedicine: DEA requires positive identity for controlled-substance prescribing over video calls
  - No competitive awareness in healthcare vertical
- **Financial services:** more fragmented
  - Multiple existing vendor relationships create complex integration challenges
  - iFood Brazil (10M deliveries/day) exploring partnership for fraud + agentic commerce
  - Travel fintech pilot: SEC-registered broker-dealer with novel high-value booking approach
- **Geolocation verification strategy**
  - GPS data triggers credential step-up (US license outside country requires passport)
  - $10K hardware cost to spoof GPS creates meaningful fraud barrier
  - Timestamp analysis detects location replay attacks
