### Background & Product Evolution

- Consumer internet team (Facebook timeline, Instagram stories) pivoting to CRM
- Started with AI presentations product at Greylock
  - Grew 50–100 users/day initially
  - Exploded to 2M users/month after adding AI generation
  - Series A & B with Lightspeed at peak
- **Pivot drivers:**
  1. AI presentations were "terrible" quality
  2. Missing relationship context for enterprise-grade work
  3. Leadership becoming detached from customer truth as company scaled
- **Vision:** Reimagine CRM as the authoritative source of customer truth
  - Sales reps working in it
  - Engineers getting roadmap influence
  - Marketing determining website messaging
- **Strategy:** Capture startups early, retain best ones through growth, then use brand to attack legacy enterprises

### Competitive Positioning & Market Dynamics

- **Traditional CRM incumbents** (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, Airtable):
  - Same fundamental architecture
  - Lack proper data for forecasting & AI workflows
  - Migration pitch: "Never fill out CRM again" + natural language automations
- **New AI CRM competitors:**
  - **Monaco:** Wedge-first approach, loses customers when they need real CRM at scale
  - **Sequoia-backed competitor:** Built on graph databases, too slow
  - **Revo (Kleiner):** Mile wide / inch deep, $4M/month burn, product not hardened
- **Enterprise migration challenges acknowledged:**
  - Deep workflow integrations
  - Downstream application dependencies
  - Regulatory/security hurdles with PII
- **Migration advantages:**
  - Automated data transfer agent (day-long vs months-long process)
  - Natural language automation builder with Claude API
  - Workforce already familiar with AI tools
