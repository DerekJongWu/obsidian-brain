## Partnership Overview & Context

- U.S. Bank has invested in two Fin Capital funds since 2021
- Quarterly leadership calls focus on specific topics
- Today's session dedicated to anti-fraud and security
- Two-way thought leadership exchange between VC and bank perspectives

## AI-Driven Cybersecurity Market Transformation

- 80%+ increase in security spending planned across financial services
- 88% of FIs planning further increases; $50M+ spend threshold common
- 60% of bank budgets tied to legacy maintenance, 40% innovation
  - Cyber is the fastest-growing category within innovation spend
- AI dual impact:
  - New attack vectors requiring AI-powered defenses
  - AI-powered solutions displacing incumbents
- AI component in attacks: 30% (2024) → 90% (2025) → approaching 100% (2026)
- TAM expanding to $2 trillion by 2025 (McKinsey)

## Human Attack Surface Protection

- Second-channel verification failing due to deepfake advancement
- Security training evolving toward AI-powered, role-tailored platforms
- Deepfake detection companies gaining traction:
  - [[Reality Defender]]: embedding into Zoom/Teams/Webex for real-time detection
  - Get Real, Pinpoint: competing in RFPs, sometimes replacing incumbents
  - Focus on scalability, easy integration, human-in-loop factors
- Email security and insider threat prevention:
  - [[Abnormal AI]] leading email defense space
  - [[Vanish ID]] scanning black market for employee credential exposure
  - [[Spectrum Security]] / Symphony creating holistic identity graphs for access management
  - [[Opti]] focusing on IAM/IGA lifecycle enforcement

## Agent Identity Management (Emerging Category)

- Rise of agentic AI creating new identity challenges:
  - Should agents have separate identities from deploying users?
  - Geographic separation issues (agent in California, user in Virginia)
  - Access control for autonomous agents
- Key players raising significant venture funding:
  - [[Keycard]]: context-aware, just-in-time certification
  - [[Ambit]]: focused on credential provisioning for agents
  - [[Asterisk]], [[Oasis]]: lifecycle management for non-human identities
- Ephemeral identity tokens for specific tasks, automatic credential expiration

## Transaction Fraud & Dispute Innovation

- Payment fraud shifting from anonymous behavior detection to recipient/network analysis
- [[Tunic Pay]] (Fin portfolio):
  - Works with HSBC, Barclays; expanding to U.S.
  - Focuses on payee validation with behavioral/network intelligence
  - Relevant for Zelle reimbursement disputes, mule account detection
- Consolidation trend across payment instruments (card, wire, ACH, RTP):
  - [[Sardine]]: identity/device intelligence focus
  - [[Unit 21]]: investigation workflow automation
  - [[Assley]]: centralized decisioning across fraud/risk workflows
- Dispute management modernization:
  - [[Case App]]: evidence collection automation, moving upmarket
  - 90-day investigation timelines creating customer friction
  - Consortium fraud signals and dispute trust scoring emerging

## Behavioral Analytics & Fraud Intelligence

- [[Neural ID]] (exited to Experian): typing cadence, mouse movement detection
- [[Call Sign]]: similar behavioral biometrics plus MFA integration
- Synthetic identity detection focus due to AI-generated account creation speed
- Open source intelligence integration trend:
  - [[VisionHeight]]: graphs adversary infrastructure, domains, certificates
  - [[Overwatch]]: graphs attacker identities, dark web chatter
  - [[Spec]]: behavioral data linking across sessions
  - [[HECA]]: consumer identity graphing from open web data
- Fraud operating across institutional boundaries, disproportionately exposing regional / community banks

## Key Questions & Follow-ups

- Integration vs. replacement strategy for existing toolchains
- Maturity assessment: cybersecurity has funding momentum but lighter enterprise traction
- Early adoption vs. waiting for incumbent vendor features
- Convergence opportunities between cyber, fraud, HR, workplace violence prevention
- Sensor integration potential (wearables, biometrics) for high-value transactions
